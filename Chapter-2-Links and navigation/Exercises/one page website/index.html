üé¨ Plot Overview

Oppenheimer is a biopic and it tells the life story of J. Robert Oppenheimer, a theoretical physicist, and his contributions towards the creation of an atomic bomb during the World War II. The main storyline focuses on Oppenheimer from the beginning of his educational career to the launching of the Manhattan Project (the first nuclear weapons development program) and the many consequences, both social and political, that he faced afterward.The film covers the moral struggles he faced due to the devastation that the weapon caused, alongside all of the character assassination he faced during the 1954 loyalty hearings that stripped him of his security clearances, to reflect the extent of scrutiny that he faced.

  1. J. Robert Oppenheimer: A brilliant physicist whose leadership in the Manhattan Project led to the creation of the atomic bomb. The film explores his intellectual prowess, personal relationships, and the profound guilt he experiences post-war.
  2. Kitty Oppenheimer: Oppenheimer's wife, who provides emotional support but also grapples with her own struggles amidst the political turmoil.
  3. Lewis Strauss: A senior figure in the U.S. Atomic Energy Commission, whose complex relationship with Oppenheimer adds tension to the narrative.
  4. General Leslie Groves: The military leader overseeing the Manhattan Project, working closely with Oppenheimer to achieve their objectives.
  5. Jean Tatlock: A psychiatrist and Oppenheimer's former lover, representing his ties to leftist politics and personal conflicts.
